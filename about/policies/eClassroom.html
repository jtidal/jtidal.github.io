Conditions of Use

The Library classroom (Rm. L540), has 30 computer workstations and is reserved for computer-related instruction, including:

  • The Library's program of course-related research and information literacy instruction for students
  • Library workshops for faculty and students
  • Academic course sections requiring a computer classroom (limited to one or two such meetings per course section per semester)
  • Faculty/staff training using the Internet and other electronic resources.

Due to our growing library instruction program, please note that we may be unable to schedule classes other than our own, especially during the busiest times of the semester. The library classroom may NOT be used for an entire course. Final authorization for classroom use is at the discretion of the library administration.

Making a Reservation

Class visits to the library are by appointment only. Read our complete class visits policy.

Please use this form to make an appointment to use the library classroom. Please note that our classroom fills up during the semester, so it's best to submit your request as early as possible. Please do not assume that the classroom is available until you have received confirmation from us.

Classroom Procedures

Please visit the library's administrative office, Rm. L544, during daytime hours, or speak to the reference librarian on the 4th floor after 5:00pm to unlock the door to the classroom. The library department cannot provide a technician or other technical support for non-library classes or programs.

Instructor Responsibilities

  • No food or drink is allowed in the classroom.
  • The instructor must remain with the class at all times. Do not leave the room unsupervised.
  • Do not load software of any kind onto the classroom computers.
  • The instructor is responsible for the security of all classroom hardware and software. Please be aware of what students are doing to prevent any abuse of equipment.
  • There is no printing in the library classroom.
  • At the end of the class please turn off the projectors and shut down all computers (instructions are posted on the teacher podium).
  • Please leave the room at the scheduled time, turn off the lights, and lock the door.
  • The instructor bears the ultimate responsibility to maintain the classroom in accord with library and college policies. All instructors must be familiar with the CUNY computer users' responsibilities.
